The cadence runs on five tiers: daily money-sense, the weekly review, the monthly close, the quarterly recalibration, and the annual reset. Tap any month to see what it triggers. Gold months hold a quarterly review.

The 12-Month View

The rhythm. Daily is 10 passive minutes. Weekly is 15. Monthly close is 30. The quarterly review is the 90 minutes that runs everything. The annual reset is a December half-day.

Wealth Dashboard: One screen, four numbers.

A forecast you can't see is a forecast you won't run. Enter your numbers and the dashboard returns the one figure that tells you whether the system is working: months to F.I.N.E. (Financially Independent, Next Endeavor) at your current trajectory.

Your Inputs

Quarterly Review Runbook: 90 minutes, five sections.

The business review for your money. Work the five sections in order, refresh the dashboard, write down the one or two decisions the quarter requires, and close the laptop. Generate your copy-pasteable checklist at the bottom.

The Five Sections

1. Cash Flow (15 min)

  • Did the plan hold this quarter? What broke the pattern?
  • Did every automated transfer fire on schedule?
  • Is the cash bucket still at its target months of expenses?
Refresh: the monthly-close number on your dashboard.

2. Investments (20 min)

  • Has anything drifted past its rebalancing band?
  • Are contributions on pace for the annual limit?
  • Does asset location still make sense (Lesson 10)?
Refresh: the net-worth and drift figures.

3. Taxes (20 min)

  • What's the estimated-payment picture for this quarter?
  • Any moves before the quarter closes (harvesting, conversions)?
  • In Q4: is the year-end Roth conversion window open for you?
Refresh: the Tax Calendar tab dates.

4. Structure (15 min)

  • Is the LLC / entity setup still right for where the income is now (Lesson 8)?
  • Any account or beneficiary change needed?
Refresh: your structure notes.

5. Decisions (20 min)

  • What one or two calls does this quarter actually require?
  • Which Playground do you revisit before next quarter?
Refresh: the All-Playgrounds Map tab.

Tax Calendar: The year on rails, not surprises.

Every estimated-payment date, contribution deadline, and the year-end conversion window placed on one view. Toggle the owner view to surface the LLC and Solo 401(k) dates from Lessons 8 and 9. Dates are 2026 U.S. federal references; confirm specifics with your tax professional.

Year-at-a-Glance Tax Calendar

How to use it. At each quarterly review, glance at the next two dates on this list and drop the ones that apply to you into your calendar. The estimated-payment and conversion decisions belong inside a scheduled slot, not in an April scramble.
The Q4 window. The richest decisions cluster in the back half of the year: the year-end Roth conversion window, the Solo 401(k) deferral election, tax-loss harvesting, and charitable timing. The annual reset in December is where they get made.
